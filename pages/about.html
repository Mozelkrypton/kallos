Article 1 — Identity

Who we are &
why we exist

Kallos Youth Empowerment is a Christ-centered, youth-focused nonprofit committed to the holistic empowerment of young people aged 18–30 — spiritually, socially, and economically.

Vision

To raise a generation of Christ-centered, empowered, and purpose-driven young people who transform society with influence, integrity, and innovation.

Mission

To empower and equip young people (18–30 years) spiritually, socially, and economically through discipleship, mentorship, leadership development, and practical initiatives that inspire generational impact and Kingdom influence.

Article 5 — Philosophy

A movement and an organization

KYE understands that sustainable youth transformation requires clear structures, accountability, and intentional leadership development. Therefore, KYE operates both as a movement and a well-structured organization — ensuring excellence, impact, and long-term growth.

We are not merely program-driven. We are people-driven — forming disciples who carry the mission beyond the walls of any gathering.

"For we are God's handiwork, created in Christ Jesus to do good works, which God prepared in advance for us to do." — Ephesians 2:10

Articles 3 & 5 — Governance

How we are governed

KYE is governed by a Board of Advisors/Trustees of 5–11 members, including at least one spiritual leader, one finance professional, one legal/compliance advisor, and one youth development leader. Board members serve 3-year terms (renewable once, maximum 6 consecutive years).

Board Officers

Leadership of the Board

  • Chair — Presides over meetings; leads Board evaluations; represents the Board.
  • Vice-Chair — Acts in the Chair's absence; supports governance quality.
  • Secretary — Keeps minutes, maintains records, manages notices.
  • Treasurer — Oversees financial stewardship, reporting, and audits.
Executive Leadership

Operational Leadership

  • Founder / Director — Vision bearer; responsible for mission execution, spiritual leadership, strategy, and Board reporting.
  • Deputy / Associate Director — Oversees operations, HR, departmental alignment, program delivery, and compliance.
Article 5 — Committees

Standing Committees

  • Finance & Audit — Budgets, controls, audits, donor compliance.
  • Programs & Ministry — Curriculum, discipleship, outreach quality.
  • Fundraising & Partnerships — Grants, donors, churches, NGOs.
  • HR & Safeguarding — Recruitment, volunteers, child protection.
  • MEL — Indicators, data quality, impact reports.
Article 8 — Decision-Making

Voting Thresholds

  • Routine matters — Simple majority of quorum (50% + 1).
  • Charter/Bylaws changes — Two-thirds (2/3) of the Board.
  • Board member removal — Two-thirds (2/3) vote.
  • Dissolution — Two-thirds (2/3) vote + legal compliance.

Article 11 — Safeguarding

Our commitment to safe spaces

KYE is committed to protecting every child, youth, and vulnerable adult who engages with our programs. Safeguarding is not optional — it is foundational to our ministry.

◈

Two-Adult Rule

No staff or volunteer works alone with a minor or vulnerable person.

◈

Mandatory Reporting

All incidents are reported through confidential channels with non-retaliation protection.

◈

Mandatory Training

All staff, volunteers, and leaders complete safeguarding training with periodic refreshers.

◈

Mental Health Support

Basic psychosocial support, referral to qualified professionals, and crisis response protocol.

◈

Consent & Privacy

Photography, media, and personal data require documented consent from participants.

◈

No Coercion

Participation in spiritual activities is never a condition for accessing KYE services.

Articles 16 & 17 — Accountability

Transparency & stewardship

Financial Controls

All payments require at least two signatories. Duties are segregated between initiator, approver, and reconciler. An independent annual audit is conducted as required by law and donors.

Anti-Fraud & Whistleblowing

KYE maintains zero tolerance for fraud, corruption, and abuse. Protected whistleblowing channels are available with guaranteed non-retaliation for good-faith disclosures.

Conflict of Interest

Board members and staff make annual conflict-of-interest declarations. Conflicted parties recuse themselves from related decisions. All related-party transactions require Board approval.

Annual Impact Report

KYE publishes a non-confidential annual impact report covering program outcomes, financial summaries, and organizational growth metrics — available to all stakeholders.
